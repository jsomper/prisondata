UK prisons in crisis
By James Somper

UK prisons are on the verge of crisis. Since 2000, the prison population has surged in size amid reduced staff numbers and budget cuts.

In August, the President of the Prison Govenors Assocation warned that prison officers across the country faced a "toxic mix" of pressures amidst "perverse" Government reform.


In an open letter, Andrea Butt said: “The recent increase in concerted indiscipline is of grave concern.”

“We know many prisons are in crisis and I deliberately use that term, because it can't be dressed up in any other way,” she added.


She also added that despite years of insistence by prison governors, the Ministry of Justice had done “nothing tangible” to address those concerns.

Violence is also a major issue in the “toxic mix” which has engulfed the prison system.


In April of this year, a report by the the Council of Europe’s Committee for the Prevention of Torture and Inhuman and Degrading Treatment or Punishment (CPT) raised “serious concerns over the lack of safety for inmates and staff in prisons in England.”

Specifically, the report cited “prison violence spiralling out of control, poor regimes and chronic overcrowding”.
